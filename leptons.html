Leptons

An important group containing the electron and neutrinos

There are 6 leptons, and their 6 antiparticles.




Electron - e-

Mass: 0.511 MeV/c2
Charge: -1
Spin: 1/2
Generation: 1
Year and location of discovery: J.J. Thompson, England, 1897


Electron Neutrino - ve

Mass: <2.5eV/c2
Charge: 0
Spin: 1/2
Generation: 1
Year and location of discovery: Clyde Cowan + Frederick Reines, 1956


Muon - μ

Mass: 105.7eV/c2
Charge: -1
Spin: 1/2
Generation: 2
Year and location of discovery: Carl D. Anderson + Seth Neddermeyer, 1936


Muon Neutrino - vμ

Mass: <6eV/c2
Charge: 0
Spin: 1/2
Generation: 2
Year and location of discovery: Leon Lederman, Melvin Schwartz + Jack Steinberger, 1962


Tau - τ

Mass: 1.777 GeV/c2
Charge: -1
Spin: 1/2
Generation: 3
Year and location of discovery: Martin Lewis Perl, 1975


Tau Neutrino - vτ

Mass: <15eV/c2
Charge: 0
Spin: 1/2
Generation: 3
Year and location of discovery: Fermilab, 2000



The neutrinos are weird. They have a very small mass, and can only interact with the rest of the universe through the weak nuclear force - ie only when particles decay. They are important because of their role in stars, where they are produced amongst the fusion of elements in stars. Billions are produced and they fly out of the star at just under the speed of light (roughly 300,000,000 ms-1). From the Sun, about 65 billion neutrinos pass through every square centimeter on Earth every second, and almost all of them fly all the way through the planet and out the other side!
