Quarks


The ultimate building blocks of hadrons

Quarks are the constituents of all hadrons, and are essential to all atoms.
There are 6 quarks, and also 6 antiquarks, which simply have an opposite charge to their matter counterparts.




Up Quark

Mass: 2.3 MeV/c2
Charge: +2/3
Spin: 1/2
Generation: 1st
Year and location of discovery: SLAC, 1968
Fact: The lightest of all quarks.


Down Quark

Mass: 4.8 MeV/c2
Charge: -1/3
Spin: 1/2
Generation: 1st
Year and locaion of discovery: SLAC, 1968
Fact: Along with the up quark, this particle makes up protons and neutrons, the constituents of atomic nuclei.


Charm Quark

Mass: 1.275 GeV/c2
Charge: +2/3
Spin: 1/2
Generation: 2nd
Year and location of discovery: SLAC / BNL, 1974


Strange Quark

Mass: 95 MeV/c2
Charge: -1/3
Spin: 1/2
Generation: 2nd
Year and location of discovery: SLAC, 1968
Fact: The strangeness of a strange quark is -1, but the strangeness of an anti-strange quark is +1!
