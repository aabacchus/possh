Bosons

The force carriers of the universe

These 6 particles are essential to our world, a universal glue, or fuel.




Photon - γ

Interactions: Electromagnetic Force
Mass: 0
Charge: 0
Spin: 1
Theorized by: Albert Einstein
Fact: Gauge Boson. Electromagnetic Radiation is also known as light.


Gluon - g

Interactions: Strong Nuclear Force
Mass: 0
Charge: 0
Spin: 1
Year and location of discovery: DESY, 1978
Fact: Gauge Boson. Holds quarks together inside hadrons.


Z0 Boson - Z0

Interactions: Weak Nuclear Force
Mass: 91.1876 GeV/c2
Charge: 0
Spin: 1
Year and location of discovery: CERN, 1983
Fact: Gauge Boson. Responsible for decaying and radioactivity.


W± Boson - W±

Interactions: Weak Nuclear Force
Mass: 80.385 GeV/c2
Charge: ±1
Spin: 1
Year and location of discovery: CERN, 1983
Fact: Gauge Boson. The same as the Z boson, a particle with different charge to open up more possibilities for particle decays.


Higgs Boson - H

Interactions: Higgs Field (explains why certain particles have mass)
Mass: 125.09 GeV/c2
Charge: 0
Spin: 0
Year and location of discovery: CERN, 2012
Fact: Scalar Boson.



Here is a good graph that shows the relative strength of each of the forces as energy rises. You see that Gravity is very weak at low energies while the Strong force is very strong, but the plan is that all the forces unify at certain energies, and it is this that is the 'Theory of Everything'. On the graph this is shown as a 'TOE', and the 'GUT' is the Grand Unified Theory. You can see that the first two forces to join as energy increases are the Weak and Electromagnetic forces, to converge as the Electroweak force, a combination of the two.
