I have looked at many websites and books to find the infomation on this site. I can recommend a few sources:

  • Youtube: 'Physics Girl', 'Veritasium', 'ATLAS Experiment', 'Fermilab', 'CERN', 'MinutePhysics', and certain TED Talks.
  • Internet:
    • Wikipedia has some good facts such as mass, charge, spin etc.
    • The Particle Adventure is a brilliant source for all things subatomic. There is also a great app for both Apple and Android.
    • Physics World is a bank of infomation for every part of physics, not just particle physics.
  • There are hundreds of great books out there, I have found Frank Close's 'A Very Short Introduction to Particle Physics' very helpful indeed.
  • If you want to go beyond the area of the Standard Model and into the realm of theoretical physics, there are hundreds of great books - I recommend Michio Kaku - and a website that tells you all about it can be found here.
I hope you find these helpful. I certainly did!
